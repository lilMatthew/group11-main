The pros and cons of studying I.T.?



The Pros:


One of the biggest benefits of pursuing a career in IT is the fact that education costs in this field are relatively low. Working in information technology does not require a four year degree, meaning you can save plenty of money on college costs. Instead, the field of IT generally works on a certification system, allowing you to gain one certification at a time, pacing your money investment as you advance in your career.

Another boon to working in the IT sector is that it is still a growing industry. The U.S. continues to move away from agricultural and industrial sector jobs and more and more into the technological sphere. As your skill set improves through new certifications, more and more doors will open for you in terms of job opportunities.

Companies are always looking for network and security technicians, regardless of what field the company itself is involved in. This creates many job opportunities for those who ready themselves for such a job in advance.

Jobs in the IT sector also have the added bonus of generally being well-paid for the amount of education investment involved. With only a handful of certifications, you can find yourself in a permanent position in both the public and private sector. Government jobs in network security are readily available across the nation. These jobs offer steady pay rates, regular promotion, and benefits that can't be beat.


The Cons


One of the downsides associated with all these benefits is the fact that as the information technology industry grows, so does the number of other qualified applicants, meaning that you will have a lot of competition in the field.

Every few years, new security systems and network standards come into play, forcing you to constantly stay ahead of the learning curve. Updating and maintaining your certifications will be a constant worry, so that you can stay ahead of the cheaper, more recently trained applicants in your field.

This can make information technology a mildly stressful area to work in.

A risk that always accompanies a specialized field such as this is the difficulty in switching gears should you ever decide to leave. While students graduating with a four-year degree are able to prove that they have been given a well-rounded education, workers moving out of the IT field have only their specialized certifications to fall back on. This means that if you should ever decide to leave the industry, you'll be forced to take steps backwards in terms of pay and perhaps even go back to school to qualify for other jobs.

As you can see, there are many good and bad qualities associated with working in the information technology field. If you enjoy technology and have no problem keeping yourself up to date on recent changes, then you should find yourself in a career that can maintain your lifestyle for years to come. If, however, you plan on switching careers in the future or have trouble keeping up with the latest trends, then IT might not be the field for you. Making this decision will rely on the qualities listed above. If you consider your options and choose a career in IT, you can look forward to a bright career in your future.
